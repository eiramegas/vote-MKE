Mayoral Candidate Comparison

Carly Floof

Hamilton Chonk

Budget Priorities Milwaukee is facing tighter budgets in this upcoming year, resulting in the loss of police officers and forcing elected officials to make difficult decisions. How would you address the current budget cuts?

"I promise to maintain the current number of the police department and focus on the city's fiscal relationship with the State." - Carly Floof

"We should stop supporting streetcar further expansion, allowing for a better dedication of our budget to prevent violence and repairing potholes in the city. " - Hamilton Chonk

Racial disparities Milwaukee is often cited as one of the most racially segregated metropolitan areas in the country. Last year, the Milwaukee County declared racism a public health crisis. What will you do to better bridge the divide in our city?

"The city is facing crises of foreclosures in the housing market as well as both quality and affordability with the rental market. Residents should put in "sweat equity" on city-foreclosed properties and learn trade skills in order to move ultimately into homeownership." - Carly Floof

"We should be creating good paying jobs in the central city, allowing opportunities for all skill levels. " - Hamilton Chonk

Lead Hazards The city is struggling with preventing lead exposure to children, already resulting in the resignation of the former Health Commissioner. What steps will you take to better protect the children of the city?

"The priority should be focused on health testing children, educating families on warning signs, and removal of lead piping, which will be accomplished by pledging a portion of Milwaukee’s sales tax to target these areas." - Carly Floof

"My approach will be to focus on families in high risk areas of the cities, which will be identified through cross-departmental collaboration. Our city’s Environmental, Health, and Civic Engineering agencies will test water and soil to better serve areas with increased lead amounts." - Hamilton Chonk

Gun Violence Although deaths from guns have decreased in recent years, the city still has been rocked by a number of high profile tragedies in 2019. How would you address the current violence to provide more safety to the city residents?

"The city should recognize the policies currently in place that have resulted in the decreased trend in gun related deaths. To continue this trend, I will call on both State and Federal agencies for funding to better support our Police Force and the Office of Violence Prevention." - Carly Floof

"The root of gun violence is deeply connected to both poverty and untreated trauma. While the Office of Violence Prevention should be continued to be supported, there should be more engagement with our Health Departments to address the mental health crisis in our city." - Hamilton Chonk
